Final Conclusions
Air Quality — PM2.5 & PM10
3
  • Higher PM2.5 values in 2023 in comparison to 2024
  • Little to no change in PM10 values between 2023 and 2024
  • No change in nitrogen dioxide values after determining the speed limit from 50 km/h to 30 km/h for both Noord-West and Oud-Oost
Traffic Noise
2
  • Higher values of traffic noise in Noord-West Amsterdam with a speed limit of 30 km/h in comparison to 50 km/h
  • Slightly lower values of traffic noise in Oud-Oost Amsterdam with a speed limit of 30 km/h in comparison to 50 km/h
Livability
1
  • Livability towards the centre of Amsterdam is marginally worse in comparison to the outside parts of the city
Public Opinion
3
  • In general mixed opinions of citizens about the implementation of the 30 km/h zones at the end of 2023
  • Positive opinions are mainly about people driving more calm and safe
  • Negative opinions mainly address that more traffic jams will form due to lower speeds making people drive more aggressively
Traffic Data (GGD)
1
  • GGD measurements show less traffic in 2024 in comparison to 2023

Main research question
To what extent does the reduction of the speed limit from 50 to 30 km/h correlate with changes in PM2.5 and PM10 concentrations, and how do these findings align with the perceptions and experiences of residents both before and after the policy change, and between an area where 30 km/h has been implemented (Oud-Oost) and an area where 50 km/h remains the norm (Noord-West)?
